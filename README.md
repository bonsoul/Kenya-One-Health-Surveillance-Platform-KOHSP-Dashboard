# Kenya-One-Health-Surveillance-Platform-KOHSP-Dashboard
*Real-time zoonotic disease surveillance dashboard for Kenya's One Health framework — maps, epi curves, and case line lists across human, animal & wildlife sectors.*

### Dashboard Overview
KOHSP is a digital surveillance dashboard that helps Kenya track and respond to diseases that spread between animals and humans zoonotic diseases. These include Rift Valley Fever, Rabies, Anthrax, Brucellosis, and several others that pose serious risks to communities, livestock, and wildlife across the country.

The dashboard brings together data from human health workers, veterinary officers, and wildlife rangers into a single platform, reflecting Kenya's *"One Health"* approach the recognition that human, animal, and environmental health are deeply connected.

### What it does:
Health officers at the national, county, and sub-county level can log in to see a live map of active outbreaks and disease clusters across Kenya's counties. They can track how many human and animal cases have been reported, monitor the speed of response teams, and receive alerts when a situation requires urgent action.
The case line list gives investigators a detailed record of every reported case — who was affected, where, when symptoms started, what disease is suspected, and what the outcome was. This level of detail is critical for understanding how an outbreak is spreading and whether the response is working.
Charts show epidemic trends over time, helping teams spot surges early. Alerts are color-coded by severity so the most critical situations are never buried.

### Who uses it:

National coordinators at the Ministry of Health, Ministry of Agriculture, and Kenya Wildlife Service get a country-wide view
County health and veterinary officers monitor their specific regions
Sub-county and facility staff report cases and track local response activities
